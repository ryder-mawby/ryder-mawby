## Ryder Mawby

Empirical economist and data analyst focused on illicit digital markets and cryptomarket intelligence. Based in Los Angeles.

My work applies structural econometrics to transaction-level data from darknet cryptomarkets — building the measurement instruments (multilingual classification pipelines, live data collection) and the analysis on top of them. Current research centers on the geography of illicit drug trade, bilateral enforcement architecture, and payment rail evolution in post-2022 cryptomarkets.

**Working paper:** [Anti-Gravity Exists: Evidence from Darknet Drug Markets](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=6608299) — PPML gravity model on 353,983 listings across 10 cryptomarkets. Standard facilitation variables (contiguity, common language, colonial ties) invert sign. Distance elasticity −1.97. MLAT enforcement coordination test: −2.54***.
